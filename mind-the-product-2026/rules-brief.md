# Rules Brief

Source: Devpost rules pasted from the registered participant view.

## Hard Dates

- Registration and submission: May 20, 2026 09:00 BST to June 20, 2026 17:00 BST.
- Judging: June 24, 2026 10:00 BST to July 1, 2026 17:00 BST.
- Winners announced: around July 6, 2026 14:00 BST by email.

## Eligibility

- Open to individuals at age of majority where they reside.
- Teams up to 4 eligible individuals.
- Organizations may enter.
- Not open where prohibited by US or local law, including Brazil, Quebec, Russia, Crimea, Cuba, Iran, North Korea, and OFAC-designated countries.

## Required Build

- Create a new working software application during the submission period.
- Build it using an AI Builder such as Bolt, Lovable, Replit Agent, v0, or equivalent.
- The project must be installable, consistently runnable, and match the video/text description.
- Third-party APIs, SDKs, and data are allowed only with proper authorization and license compliance.
- The project must be original and owned by the entrant/team/organization.

## Novus.ai Requirement

- Entrants are given access to Novus.ai at `https://novus.pendo.io/register`.
- Projects submitted without Novus.ai installation are ineligible.
- Submission must include a screenshot of the Novus.ai dashboard.
- Shippedness explicitly includes measurability: Novus installed and behaviors trackable.

## Submission Assets

- Publicly available deployed application URL.
- Screenshot of the Novus.ai dashboard.
- Text description explaining features and functionality.
- Demo video under 3 minutes.
- Video must show the project functioning on the target device.
- Video must be public on YouTube, Vimeo, or Youku.
- Testing access must be free and unrestricted through the judging period.
- If private, include login credentials in testing instructions.
- All materials must be in English, or include English translation.

## Judging

Stage 1 is pass/fail:

- Baseline viability.
- Reasonably fits the theme.
- Reasonably applies required APIs/SDKs featured in the hackathon.

Stage 2 is equally weighted:

- Product Thinking (25%): problem worth solving, clear user, clear job, clear why.
- Craft and Execution (25%): coherent end-to-end experience, considered UI, intentional copy.
- Originality and Ambition (25%): specific, surprising, distinctly ours.
- Shippedness (25%): stranger can land on URL and get value now; measurable with Novus.

Tie-break order starts with the first listed criterion, so Product Thinking is effectively the first tie-breaker.

## Prizes

- Gold: $5,000 USD.
- Silver: $3,000 USD.
- Bronze: $2,000 USD.
- Product Bundle by Mind the Product: 10 awards, up to $2,000 value.
- Each project can win one prize.
- Prize is subject to identity, qualification, and role verification.
- Required forms must be returned within 10 business days.

## Practical Consequences

- We must treat Novus instrumentation as a core product requirement from day one.
- The demo must show a real user getting value without us narrating around broken parts.
- The app should be public and simple to test.
- The story should lead with product judgment, not implementation novelty.
- We need a dashboard screenshot before submission, so we must generate real usage events before recording.

## Submission Positioning Notes

- Lead with the changed bottleneck: AI made building faster, but launch readiness is still scarce.
- Make Novus.ai part of the story: LaunchProof maps meaningful product actions to measurable behavior evidence.
- Keep the promise concrete: a launch readiness packet with brief, flows, risks, resilience check, evidence, and pitch export.
- Do not position the app as a full PM suite, analytics platform, or user research replacement.
- Tie every major claim back to one judging category.
- The fastest judge skim should reveal: clear user, clear workflow, public URL, Novus evidence, and useful output.
